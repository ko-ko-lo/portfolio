1. Planning and Technology Stack

Project Scope and Goals

The goal of the web application is to create a calming and user-friendly platform focused on Yin-Style Yoga. At the heart of the application is a library of yoga poses, each presented with a structure that includes step-by-step instructions for entering and exiting the pose, its effects on the body, and suggested variations to suit different needs and abilities.

In addition to the library, the app also features yoga routines, allowing users to filter routines based on specific target areas.

Together, the Yoga Pose Library, routines, and introduction form core of the application. These elements represent the “Walking Skeleton” of the project - a minimal but complete version that fulfills the essential requirements for the initial launch.

Technology Stack

Choosing the right framework for this project was a careful decision. With so many options available, I ultimately decided to go with Angular for two main reasons:

First, after starting to learn Java, I really grew to appreciate the Object-Oriented Approach. As Angular embraces this same approach, it made a natural fit for me.

The second reason is my plan to transform this app into a Progressive Web Application (PWA). Since both PWAs and Angular were developed by Google, Angular makes it especially smooth to turn an Angular application into a PWA.

For the styling, I chose SCSS because it allows me to write more efficient and maintainable CSS by using features like variables, mixins, and nesting.

Summary: Planning and Technology Stack

The Yin-Style Yoga Application is designed as a user-friendly platform featuring a well-structured Yoga Pose Library and Yoga Routines, which users can filter based on target areas. Built using Angular for its object-oriented approach and future scalability as a Progressive Web App (PWA), the app's component-based architecture ensures modularity and maintainability. Styling is handled with SCSS, providing efficient and reusable design through variables and mixins. Together, these elements create a flexible foundation for future expansions while offering a smooth user experience.

2. UX/UI Design Process

Research and Discovery Phase

Creating the Yin-Style Yoga web application started with a personal challenge: organizing my yoga routines and pose instructions in a more efficient and accessible way.

Identifying the Problem

Before creating this application, all my yoga routines and instructions were scattered across various papers, making it difficult to quickly access the information I needed. Every time I wanted to create a new routine or perform an existing one, I had to search through my "paper chaos" to find details about specific poses. This slowed down my practice and made it harder to stay organized.

The primary goal of the application was to speed up the creation and execution of yoga routines, while providing a simple, quick way to access all the information I needed about each pose.

I envisioned an organized library that would allow me to easily find, filter, and access yoga poses, saving me time and improving the flow of my practice.

Brainstorming the Product Requirements

During my brainstorming sessions, I explored a variety of functionalities that would enhance the user experience and provide real value. Initially, I dreamed big, listing features such as:

  • A Login Area with a Personal Dashboard for tracking progress.
  • A tool to create custom yoga routines tailored to the user's needs.
  • Pre-made routines and graphical videos to inspire users who are looking for guidance.

However, I knew I needed to prioritize the core functionalities for the initial release. This led to the creation of key product requirements that would lay the foundation for the application:

  1. Quick and Easy Access to Yoga Poses: The application needed a well-structured library where users could quickly find and filter yoga poses based on target areas.
  2. Silent, Self-Paced Practice: Users should be able to conduct ready-made yoga routines in a peaceful environment, without distractions.
  3. Introduction to Yin-Style Yoga: For those new to the practice, a brief introduction would offer insights into the benefits and techniques of Yin-Style Yoga.

By narrowing down these key requirements, I was able to focus on creating a product that is user-friendly and valuable from the very first version. I also kept in mind that the initial launch could serve as a foundation, with the potential to expand the app's functionality in the future.

High-Level Design Concept

In this phase, the focus was on creating a flexible, scalable structure for the Yin-Style Yoga App. The high-level design decisions revolved around visualizing the site's structure, organizing content efficiently, and setting the overall look and feel of the application.

A key consideration in the design process was ensuring that the information architecture was modular and scalable. Content Creation and Structure

The next challenge was to create content for the whole application, particularly for the Yoga Pose Library. The goal was to create content that was both clear and concise, providing all the information needed without overwhelming with unnecessary details.

Filtering Options

When designing the filtering system for the Yoga Pose Library and Yoga Routines, I wanted to strike a balance between simplicity and utility. Too many filters would overwhelm users, while too few might limit their ability to find the content that suits their needs.

Wireframes

To bring the structure of the pages to life and better understand the layout, user flows, and how various elements like text and images would interact, I created simple wireframes. These rough wireframes allowed me to explore the platform's design and functionality early on, providing a clear sense of how the web application would feel and flow without getting lost in the finer details.

High-Level Visual Design Guide

To establish a strong visual identity for the application, I began by creating a high-level design guide. This guide outlines the core elements that would define the look and feel of the application, from color schemes to typography.

The design needed to reflect the calming, meditative nature of Yin-Style Yoga. I chose a color palette with soft, soothing tones - gentle purples and blues that evoke a sense of peace and balance. The typography was selected to be clean and easy to read.

Detailed Design

The Detailed Design phase was where the vision for the Yin-Style Yoga Application took shape in a concrete, visual form. This phase was centered around building a comprehensive Design System that would not only define the look and feel of the application but also ensure consistency and scalability throughout the design. From the foundation of this system, reusable components and design patterns were created, followed by the visual designs.

Building the Foundation of Design System

The foundation of the Design System began with the creation of variables, such as the Color Collections, which ensured that the color palette remained consistent across the entire application. Using color variables, I established semantic color aliases (for example background, border, accent) to make the use of colors more efficient and meaningful throughout the design.

In addition to color, I defined variables for other key elements such as typography, number scales, border radius, elevation, and breakpoints.

Reusable Components and Patterns of the Design System

Based on the foundational variables, I began creating reusable components that would form the building blocks of the application's interface. These components included:

  • Buttons and Filters: Designed to be flexible and scalable, with various states for default, hover, and active interactions.
  • Cards: Components like the Yoga Pose Card and Training Card were designed, ensuring they were visually consistent and adaptable to different contexts.
  • Accordion: A design pattern for expanding and collapsing content.
  • Main Navigation and Footer: Design patterns for consistent navigation and branding across the app were also created.

These components were built with reusability in mind, ensuring they could be used across different sections of the application without the need for redesign or additional effort.

Yoga Pose Illustrations

As part of the Design System, I created custom illustrations for the yoga poses using Procreate. These illustrations were designed to be simple yet effective, focusing on clear representation of each pose without overwhelming detail.

Creating the Visual Designs

With the Design System in place, I moved on to the creation of the Visual Designs for the application. These designs followed the structure and flow established in the High-Level Design phase, ensuring that the user experience remained cohesive from start to finish.

The Visual Designs were created with the goal of maintaining calmness and clarity, using the variables, components and design patterns defined in the Design System.

3. Development Process

The Development Process of the Yin-Style Yoga Application was where the ideas and designs truly came to life. Having established a clear vision during the planning and design phases, I was excited to bring all of the key features together using Angular, TypeScript, HTML and SCSS.

Throughout the development journey, I aimed to create an application that is technically robust and easy to maintain. By leveraging Angular's component-based architecture, I ensured that the app remains modular, scalable, and flexible for future expansions.

Application Structure

During the transition from design to development it was time to bring all the planning and visual decisions made in the Design System to life within the actual structure of the app. Using Angular's component-based architecture, I was able to take the reusable components and patterns I had built in Figma and translate them into a modular, maintainable codebase.

The variables.scss file, for example, was a direct reflection of the Design System's Color Collections and other foundational elements, such as box shadows, radius, and number scales. This alignment ensured that the app's visual consistency - established during the design phase - would be carried over seamlessly into the code.

Core Components

In Angular I structured the app around reusable components.

  • Yoga Pose Component: This component is responsible for displaying yoga poses, dynamically rendered based on data stored in a JSON file. This component is reused throughout the library and individual routines.
  • Filtering Component: The filtering component allows users to filter based on target areas. It communicates with its parent components using Angular's input and output bindings. It is reused in the library and the routines overview page.
  • Breadcrumb Component: This component provides easy navigation for users by displaying the current path and dynamically updating based on the page or routine being viewed. It fetches yoga routine data from a JSON file to display relevant information in the breadcrumb trail.
  • Global Components: The Main Menu and Footer components are part of the app's core structure.

Routing and Navigation

The application's navigation is powered by Angular's router, enabling smooth transitions between key pages.

Dynamic routing is used to load individual yoga routines based on a URL parameter (slug), allowing users to explore specific routines in more detail. Features like scroll position restoration and anchor scrolling were also enabled to ensure a seamless and enjoyable user experience.

Data Management

All data for the app is managed using JSON files for yoga poses and routines, which are dynamically loaded through Angular's HttpClient service.

This approach not only keeps the app flexible and easy to update but also allows for future scalability, including potential back-end integration for more dynamic content management.

Styling and Theming

The styling is divided into:

  • Global Styles: These base styles and utility classes ensure consistency across the app, with variables for colors, fonts, and other core elements defined in the variables.scss file.
  • Component-Specific Styles: To maintain flexibility and prevent conflicts, each component has its own SCSS file, making the styling modular and easy to manage as the application grows.

Development Workflow

Throughout the development of the Yin-Style Yoga Application, I followed a structured workflow to ensure the project was organized, trackable, and easy to maintain.

At the heart of this process was Git for version control, with all project files stored in a GitHub repository. After the initial setup and first commit, I committed code regularly at key intervals as I developed new features and made updates. This allowed me to keep track of the project's progress and roll back changes if necessary.

To maintain a smooth workflow, I regularly tested the application on different devices and browsers during development. This ensured that features like dynamic routing, filtering components, and responsive design were functioning correctly across various environments.

As I continue to grow my development skills, I look forward to refining this workflow further by incorporating additional best practices like automated testing and continuous integration.

5. Final Reflections

Working on the Yin-Style Yoga web application has been a deeply personal and educational journey.

One of the key takeaways from this experience was learning to embrace the process rather than rushing toward the end result. Preparing the yoga pose illustrations and carefully crafting the descriptive text for each pose took far longer than I initially expected. My early attempts to speed through these tasks left me unsatisfied with the quality, which led to a restart. It became clear that the real value lay in the journey itself - learning more about each pose and finding a way to convey its essence. By slowing down, I was able to produce content I could be proud of.

A similar lesson applied to the visual design. My first attempt at the visual design wasn't quite right. It was too trendy and lacked the timeless simplicity I wanted. Although I created a prototype, I knew it wasn't ready for testing. Starting the design process again was the right decision, as it allowed me to create a design that truly felt aligned with the message I wanted the application to convey. Even with years of design experience, it's a reminder that sometimes you have to take a step back, reflect, and try again until the design feels just right.

This project also reinforced the importance of focus and prioritization. It's easy to get caught up in the excitement of new ideas and want to implement every feature at once. But I learned that focusing on the essentials - building a strong foundation and taking things step by step - is the most effective way to make meaningful progress. For me, prioritization was challenging, but it was a crucial part of ensuring that the project moved forward in a manageable and successful way.

On the development side, working with Angular was a rewarding experience. The more I learned, the more I appreciated the power and flexibility of the framework. That said, there were moments when small development challenges nearly got the better of me - like debugging a page-scrolling issue that, after hours of frustration, was solved with a simple line of code. These moments reminded me of the patience required in development, and the satisfaction that comes when a solution is finally found.

Reflecting on the entire project, I've come to realize that:

  • It's okay to take things slowly, as long as you keep moving forward.
  • It's okay to restart, as long as you learn from the process.
  • Having a vision for the product - focusing not on the minute details but on the overall feeling and purpose of the application - was what helped me stay motivated and see this project through to the end.

Summary: Final Reflections

Working on the Yin-Style Yoga web application has been a profoundly personal and educational journey, teaching me invaluable lessons about both the creative process and my growth as a designer and developer. Embracing the journey rather than rushing toward the end result helped me create quality content I could truly be proud of. I learned the importance of focus and prioritization, realizing that building a strong foundation step by step is essential for meaningful progress. Throughout the development process, even when faced with challenges, I found joy in the solutions and deepened my appreciation for the craft. Ultimately, this project has enriched my technical skills while nurturing my understanding of perseverance and creativity.
