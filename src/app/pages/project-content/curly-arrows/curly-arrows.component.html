1. Purpose & Goal

Curly Arrows began with a keynote.

To make my presentation feel more personal and engaging, I hand-drew small illustrations, including playful, imperfect arrows that added character and flow. While preparing, I realized: why not share these? Others might want to add that same expressive touch to their own work.

The goal of Curly Arrows is to provide clean, hand-drawn arrows that stand out with personality, perfect for enriching user flows, websites, or presentations where standard arrows might go unnoticed.

More than just sharing assets, I wanted to build something useful, beautiful, and simple to use: a tiny plugin that brings joy into the design process.

2. Features & Visual Design

Every arrow in Curly Arrows starts with a hand-drawn sketch. This gives each one a natural rhythm and personality. These sketches capture the playful imperfections that make the arrows feel expressive, dynamic, and a little unexpected.

To turn these sketches into interactive assets, I convert them into vector paths. This allows for flexibility inside Figma, particularly when it comes to stroke width. The plugin includes a stroke slider, giving users control over the thickness of the arrow lines, from 1px to 10px. Adjustments are visible instantly in the plugin preview.

Curly Arrows also comes with a curated palette of six colors, plus a deep charcoal gray. These options let users experiment quickly inside the plugin, while keeping the door open for full customization: once inserted into Figma, arrows behave like any native vector - users can change color, or apply any style they like.

The UI is intentionally simple and designed to make selection and customization effortless. It includes:

  • A live preview of each arrow
  • A color picker with hover labels
  • A stroke width slider with real-time updates

3. Development Insights

One of the trickiest parts was working with SVG paths exported from Figma. I wanted to give users the ability to change stroke width dynamically, but many of my initial arrow designs didn't include proper stroke attributes, especially if the paths had advanced stroke settings or were flattened during export.

I had to learn how to prepare the SVGs so that they remained editable, and how to inject stroke and stroke-width values via code before inserting them into Figma.

4. Final Reflections

I'm really happy with how the arrows turned out - they feel playful, expressive, and fun to use. I also like the clean, simple UI that allows users to pick from curated colors and adjust stroke width without friction.

On the technical side, this project helped me deepen my understanding of the Figma Plugin API, especially how to work with SVG paths and inject dynamic attributes like color and stroke.

Curly Arrows is now part of my growing family of playful plugins, alongside Smiley Express and Pattern Hearts.

I've already received feedback about adding animation features. Let's see where this goes next ....

Thank you for taking the time to explore this little project.
