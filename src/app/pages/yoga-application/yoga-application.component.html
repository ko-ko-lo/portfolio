1. Planning and Technology Stack

Project Scope and Goals

The goal of the web application is to create a calming and user-friendly platform focused on Yin-Style Yoga. At the heart of the application is a library of yoga poses, each presented with a thoughtful structure that includes step-by-step instructions for entering and exiting the pose, its effects on the body, and suggested variations to suit different needs and abilities.

In addition to the library, the app also features yoga routines, allowing users to filter routines based on specific target areas.

Together, the Yoga Pose Library, routines, and introduction form core of the application. These elements represent the “Walking Skeleton” of the project - a minimal but complete version that fulfills the essential requirements for the initial launch.

Technology Stack

Choosing the right framework for this project was a careful decision. With so many options available, I ultimately decided to go with Angular for two main reasons:

First, after starting to learn Java, I really grew to appreciate the Object-Oriented Approach. As Angular embraces this same approach, it made a natural fit for me.

The second reason is my plan to transform this app into a Progressive Web Application (PWA). Since both PWAs and Angular were developed by Google, Angular makes it especially smooth to turn an Angular application into a PWA.

For the styling, I chose SCSS because it allows me to write more efficient and maintainable CSS by using features like variables, mixins, and nesting.

Summary: Planning and Technology Stack

The Yin-Style Yoga Application is designed as a user-friendly platform featuring a well-structured Yoga Pose Library and Yoga Routines, which users can filter based on target areas. Built using Angular for its object-oriented approach and future scalability as a Progressive Web App (PWA), the app's component-based architecture ensures modularity and maintainability. Styling is handled with SCSS, providing efficient and reusable design through variables and mixins. Together, these elements create a flexible foundation for future expansions while offering a smooth user experience.

2. UX/UI Design Process

Research and Discovery Phase

Creating the Yin-Style Yoga web application started with a personal challenge: organizing my yoga routines and pose instructions in a more efficient and accessible way. I wanted to develop a solution that not only worked for me but could also serve a wider audience of people who are passionate about yoga. This phase helped me define the core problems, understand the target audience, and set the key requirements for the application.

Identifying the Problem

Before creating this application, all my yoga routines and instructions were scattered across various papers, making it difficult to quickly access the information I needed. Every time I wanted to create a new routine or perform an existing one, I had to search through my "paper chaos" to find details about specific poses. This slowed down my practice and made it harder to stay organized.

The primary goal of the application was to speed up the creation and execution of yoga routines, while providing a simple, quick way to access all the information I needed about each pose. I envisioned an organized library that would allow me to easily find, filter, and access yoga poses, saving me time and improving the flow of my practice.

Target Group Analysis

As I moved forward with the idea, I considered who else might benefit from this web application. I conducted a target group analysis by asking myself key questions about potential users:

  • Who is the application for?
  • What are their needs?
  • What are the most common use cases?

From this analysis, I identified the core target group:

The Yin-Style Yoga application is thoughtfully designed to welcome a diverse audience, ranging from young adults to seniors, especially those who have a basic understanding of yoga. The ideal users are individuals who spend long hours sitting or face high levels of stress in their everyday lives. They are likely seeking a soothing, physical practice to help them find balance amidst their busy routines and nurture both their physical and mental well-being.
The application is accessible on various devices, including desktops and tablets for practicing yoga, as well as smartphones for those moments when users want to explore poses or browse our library on the go.

Brainstorming the Product Requirements

During my brainstorming sessions, I explored a variety of functionalities that would enhance the user experience and provide real value. Initially, I dreamed big, listing features such as:

  • A Login Area with a Personal Dashboard for tracking progress.
  • A tool to create custom yoga routines tailored to the user's needs.
  • A Yoga Pose Library with detailed instructions and illustrations.
  • Pre-made routines and graphical videos to inspire users who are looking for guidance.

However, I knew I needed to prioritize the core functionalities for the initial release. This led to the creation of key product requirements that would lay the foundation for the application:

  1. Quick and Easy Access to Yoga Poses: The application needed a well-structured library where users could quickly find and filter yoga poses based on target areas.
  2. Silent, Self-Paced Practice: Users should be able to conduct ready-made yoga routines in a peaceful environment, without distractions.
  3. Introduction to Yin-Style Yoga: For those new to the practice, a brief introduction would offer insights into the benefits and techniques of Yin-Style Yoga.

By narrowing down these key requirements, I was able to focus on creating a product that is user-friendly and valuable from the very first version. I also kept in mind that the initial launch could serve as a foundation, with the potential to expand the app's functionality in the future.

High-Level Design Concept

In this phase, the focus was on creating a flexible, scalable structure for the Yin-Style Yoga App, ensuring that users would have a smooth, intuitive experience. The high-level design decisions revolved around visualizing the site's structure, organizing content efficiently, and setting the overall look and feel of the application. The goal was to ensure that the app would not only meet users' current needs but could also grow and expand in the future.

Scalable Information Architecture

A key consideration in the design process was ensuring that the information architecture was modular and scalable. As the application evolves, I plan to add more content, such as Breathing Techniques and Meditation Sessions, which should be integrated seamlessly.

The design began by visualizing a Sitemap that would allow for future growth. This sitemap outlines the main sections - such as the Yoga Pose Library, Yoga Routines, and future sections for Breathing and Meditation.

Content Creation and Structure

The next challenge was to create content for the whole application, particularly for the Yoga Pose Library. The goal was to create content that was both clear and concise, giving users all the information they need without overwhelming them with unnecessary details.

Filtering Options

When designing the filtering system for the Yoga Pose Library and Yoga Routines, I wanted to strike a balance between simplicity and utility. Too many filters would overwhelm users, while too few might limit their ability to find the content that suits their needs.

Wireframes

To bring the structure of the pages to life and better understand the layout, user flows, and how various elements like text and images would interact, I created simple wireframes. These rough wireframes allowed me to explore the platform's design and functionality early on, providing a clear sense of how the web application would feel and flow without getting lost in the finer details.

High-Level Visual Design Guide

To establish a strong visual identity for the application, I began by creating a high-level design guide. This guide outlines the core elements that would define the look and feel of the application, from color schemes to typography.

The design needed to reflect the calming, meditative nature of Yin-Style Yoga. I chose a color palette with soft, soothing tones - gentle purples and blues that evoke a sense of peace and balance. The typography was selected to be clean and easy to read.

Detailed Design

The Detailed Design phase was where the vision for the Yin-Style Yoga Application took shape in a concrete, visual form. This phase was centered around building a comprehensive Design System that would not only define the look and feel of the application but also ensure consistency and scalability throughout the design. From the foundation of this system, reusable components and design patterns were created, followed by the visual designs.

Building the Foundation of Design System

The foundation of the Design System began with the creation of variables, such as the Color Collections, which ensured that the color palette remained consistent across the entire application. Using color variables, I established semantic color aliases (for example background, border, accent) to make the use of colors more efficient and meaningful throughout the design.

In addition to color, I defined variables for other key elements such as typography, number scales, border radius, elevation, and breakpoints.

Reusable Components and Patterns of the Design System

Based on the foundational variables, I began creating reusable components that would form the building blocks of the application's interface. These components included:

  • Buttons and Filters: Designed to be flexible and scalable, with various states for default, hover, and active interactions.
  • Cards: Components like the Yoga Pose Card and Training Card were designed, ensuring they were visually consistent and adaptable to different contexts.
  • Accordion: A design pattern for expanding and collapsing content.
  • Main Navigation and Footer: Design patterns for consistent navigation and branding across the app were also created.

These components were built with reusability in mind, ensuring they could be used across different sections of the application without the need for redesign or additional effort, saving time in designing and maintaining consistency.

Yoga Pose Illustrations

As part of the Design System, I created custom illustrations for the yoga poses using Procreate. These illustrations were designed to be simple yet effective, focusing on clear representation of each pose without overwhelming detail.

Creating the Visual Designs

With the Design System in place, I moved on to the creation of the Visual Designs for the application. These designs followed the structure and flow established in the High-Level Design phase, ensuring that the user experience remained cohesive from start to finish.

The Visual Designs were created with the goal of maintaining calmness and clarity, using the variables, components and design patterns defined in the Design System. Each screen was carefully crafted to guide the user through the application in a way that felt intuitive.

Prototyping and Testing

Typically, in my UX/UI Design process, I include a dedicated Prototyping and Testing phase. This is where I would normally create interactive prototypes to test the user flow, design interactions, and gather feedback before moving into development. However, since this is a personal project, I decided to skip this phase in favor of diving directly into the development process.

My familiarity with the user needs and content, combined with the flexibility to iterate during development, allowed me to move forward confidently. While I value the importance of prototyping and testing in a typical project, this approach allowed me to focus on hands-on learning and quickly bring my vision to life.

If you're interested in learning how I conduct Usability Testing and how it can improve the user experience, you can read an article I wrote on the topic here: Usability Testing Made Easy in 4 Steps.

Summary: UX/UI Process

The UX/UI Design Process for the Yin-Style Yoga App began with a thorough Research and Discovery phase, where I identified key user needs and defined the application's target audience. From there, I moved into the High-Level Design phase, focusing on creating a scalable and flexible structure for the app's information architecture and content. During the Detailed Design phase, I developed a comprehensive Design System with reusable components, custom illustrations, and the Visual Designs which ensure a consistent and harmonious user experience.

3. Development Process

The Development Process of the Yin-Style Yoga Application was where the ideas and designs truly came to life. Having established a clear vision during the planning and design phases, I was excited to bring all of the key features together using a combination of Angular, TypeScript, HTML and SCSS.

Throughout the development journey, I aimed to create an application that is not only visually appealing but also technically robust and easy to maintain. By leveraging Angular's component-based architecture, I ensured that the app remains modular, scalable, and flexible for future expansions.

Application Structure

During the transition from design to development it was time to bring all the planning and visual decisions made in the Design System to life within the actual structure of the app. Using Angular's component-based architecture, I was able to take the reusable components and patterns I had built in Figma and translate them into a modular, maintainable codebase.

The variables.scss file, for example, was a direct reflection of the Design System's Color Collections and other foundational elements, such as box shadows, radius, and number scales. This alignment ensured that the app's visual consistency - established during the design phase - would be carried over seamlessly into the code.

Core Components

Just as I had carefully organized the design components and patterns in Figma, I followed a similar approach in Angular by structuring the app around reusable components.
