Roots in Art and Design

From an early age, art became my way of expressing feelings and thoughts that were often too complex for words. It offered me a space where I could explore my emotions, meditate, and develop personally. Art has always been a form of self-expression and growth for me, shaping not only my creativity but also my approach to life.

This deep connection to art naturally led me to pursue a formal education in Product Design, where I embraced the principles of the Bauhaus philosophy. At the core of this philosophy is the belief that, to create something meaningful, you must first understand its materials, behaviors and processes. This mindset was instilled in me throughout my studies, particularly in the traditional craft of glassmaking. I learned to shape glass with my own hands, understanding its nuances and limitations before translating that knowledge into design. This approach - of deeply knowing before creatingâ€”has remained a guiding principle in all aspects of my work.

Whether I'm designing physical products, creating digital art, or writing code, I always start by exploring the underlying materials, tools, and possibilities. It's a mindset that reminds me to dig deep, to understand, and to craft with intention.

Becoming a UX/UI Designer

After completing my studies in product design, I initially began my career in that field, but soon realized that the opportunities for growth - both professionally and personally - were limited. This led me to explore new paths, and while still working full-time as a product designer, I began studying graphic design in search of fresh challenges and new opportunities to develop my skills.

It wasn't long before I turned my attention to software development. The ever-growing demand for developers and the continuous opportunity for learning drew me in. I've always had a strong desire to keep learning and the systematic nature of development fascinated me. Just as I wanted to understand the craft of glassmaking in order to design meaningful products, I wanted to understand code so I could create powerful, functional digital designs.

Initially, I focused on web development. But, somewhat by chance, my journey into UX/UI Design began when my manager asked me to step in as a full-time designer - unable to find someone with both design and coding skills. It wasn't a role I had planned for, but as I dove deeper into the world of user experience and interface design, it felt like a natural fit. I combined the problem-solving skills I had developed in software with my creative background in design.

Driven by curiosity and the desire to master this new field, I dedicated myself to self-study. Through books, courses, and hands-on experience working for a variety of companies - from tech startups to consultancies and large corporations - I honed my skills in UX/UI Design.

Returning to Development

While I enjoyed the creative aspects of UX/UI Design, the problem-solving nature of software development was always calling me back. There was something deeply fulfilling about the systematic approach and the way development challenges you through logic and creativity. Over time, I realized that while design allowed me to create visually engaging experiences, it was development that truly captivated me.

For a while, I hesitated to make the shift. Although I had experience as a developer, my skills weren't up to date, and I didn't feel confident enough to apply for developer positions. But at some point, I decided it was time to make a change. I took a break from UX/UI work to focus on completing software development certifications, pushing myself to refresh and sharpen my coding skills.

Returning to development felt like coming home. It reignited my passion for problem-solving and the thrill of creating something functional and tangible. For me, development is like solving a puzzle - systematic, challenging, and deeply creative. It's the perfect blend of logic and imagination, and it allows me to take an idea and turn it into something that people can interact with and benefit from. I've come to appreciate that no matter where my journey takes me, coding remains an essential part of it.
