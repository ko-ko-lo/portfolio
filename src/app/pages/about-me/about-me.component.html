Roots in Art and Design

From an early age, art became my way of expressing feelings and thoughts that were often too complex for words. It offered me a space where I could explore my emotions, and develop personally.

This deep connection to art naturally led me to pursue a formal education in Product Design, where I embraced the principles of the Bauhaus philosophy. At the core of this philosophy is the belief that, to create something meaningful, you must first understand its materials, behaviors and processes. This mindset was instilled in me throughout my studies, particularly in the traditional craft of glassmaking. I learned to shape glass with my own hands, understanding its nuances and limitations before translating that knowledge into design.

Whether I'm designing physical or digital products, or writing code, I always start by exploring the underlying materials, tools and possibilities. It's a mindset that reminds me to dig deep, to understand and to craft with intention.

Becoming a UX/UI Designer

After completing my studies in product design, I initially began my career in that field, but soon realized that the opportunities for growth were limited. This led me to explore new paths.

I turned my attention to software development. The systematic nature of development fascinated me and the continuous opportunity for learning drew me in. Just as I wanted to understand the craft of glassmaking in order to design meaningful products, I wanted to understand code so I could design powerful, functional applications.

Initially, I focused on web development. But, somewhat by chance, my journey into UX/UI Design began. It wasn't a role I had planned for, but as I dove deeper into the world of user experience and interface design, it felt like a natural fit.

Driven by curiosity and the desire to master this new field, I dedicated myself to self-study. Through books, courses, colleagues, and hands-on experience working for a variety of companies - from tech startups to consultancies and large corporations - I honed my skills in UX/UI Design.

Returning to Development

While I enjoyed the creative aspects of UX/UI Design, the problem-solving nature of software development was always calling me back. There was something deeply fulfilling about the systematic approach and the way development challenges you through logic and creativity.

For a while, I hesitated to make the shift. Although I had experience as a developer, my skills weren't up to date. But at some point, I decided it was time to make a change. I took a break to focus on completing software development certifications, pushing myself to refresh and sharpen my coding skills.

For me, development is like solving a puzzle - systematic, challenging, and deeply creative. It's the perfect blend of logic and imagination and it allows me to take an idea and turn it into something that people can interact with and benefit from.
